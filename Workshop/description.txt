[h1]Breakdown Revisited[/h1]

See where your city's traffic actually comes from. Click any building, road, vehicle, or district and instantly see the top origin→destination pairs driving traffic through it — ranked by volume, broken down by route type, and smoothed over time with the Average mode.

[b]Breakdown Revisited[/b] is a community revival of the original [b]Breakdown[/b] by [url=https://github.com/whyoh]whyoh[/url], updated and extended for Cities: Skylines 1.21.1-f9.

[b]► Source code & changelog:[/b]
[url=https://github.com/roberto-naharro/CitiesBreakdown]github.com/roberto-naharro/CitiesBreakdown[/url]

[h1]What it shows[/h1]

[list]
[*][b]Route popup[/b] — open any WorldInfoPanel (building, road, vehicle, citizen…) to see the top-25 district pairs generating traffic through that entity
[*][b]City-wide accordion[/b] — in Traffic Routes mode, an expandable panel lists every district ranked by total connections, with per-connection breakdowns
[*][b]Route type breakdown[/b] — hover any row for a tooltip showing the split between Pedestrians, Cyclists, Private Cars, Trucks, Public Transit, and City Services
[*][b]Districts or road segments[/b] — toggle the display between district-level and road-segment-level origin/destination pairs
[*][b]Average mode[/b] — switch from live snapshot to an exponential moving average (EMA) of traffic shares accumulated over the session; smooths out traffic spikes and shows longer-term patterns
[*][b]Percentage display[/b] — Average mode shows each pair's share of total city traffic (e.g. 12.3%) with colour-coded alerts for high-traffic corridors
[/list]

[h1]How to use[/h1]

[list]
[*]Enable [b]Traffic Routes[/b] (the route-visualisation overlay) in the game's info view
[*]Click any entity — a panel appears to the right listing the top routes
[*]Use [b]Districts[/b] / [b]Roads[/b] buttons to switch display mode
[*]After a few minutes of play, the [b]Average[/b] button unlocks — click it for smoothed historical data
[*]For a city-wide overview, open [b]Traffic Routes[/b] without clicking anything — the accordion panel appears at the bottom
[/list]

[h1]Compatibility[/h1]

Compatible with [b]Cities: Skylines 1.21.1-f9[/b] and later 1.x. No Harmony dependency — uses only the standard ICities API and reflection. Should be compatible with most traffic and UI mods.

[b]No known conflicts.[/b] If you find one, please report it on the GitHub issues page.
